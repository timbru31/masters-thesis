                                                                                                                      Morten Skarstad chump2010 • 2 years ago

                                                                                                                      1+2) Keepass. Keepass supports composite logins using keyfiles. This way you can create a keyfile, combine this with your password. The keyfile you only handle locally, and only put on devices that you trust, i.e. your home computer(s), your work computer, mobile phone, whatever you like. Then use whatever cloud service you like to sync the actual database. The point is that unless you have access to the keyfile this database is going to be ridiculously hard to crack, even if you can figure out the password. Another alternative is a USB stick with a portable version of KeePass. Just remember to keep a backup copy of it at home :-)

                                                                                                                      3) Not going to happen. But you don't necessarily have to do them all at once. You just need to start somewhere. Even if you start using a pw manager and then start changing (and recording) new pws for the most critical sites and services, you are on your way. Then just take the rest as you go along.

                                                                                                                      4) If somebody gets your db, you're screwed. But the alternative, using a single pw everywhere and having it leaked also leaves you with your pants on your ankles. And having some site leak users/pws is way more likely than you leaking your db.

                                                                                                                      5) That is why you use password managers.
